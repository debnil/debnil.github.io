Hi! I'm Debnil. Here's my Twitter, LinkedIn, and CV.

I live in San Francisco and build systems at Interstellar. In my free time, I coach policy debate at Lowell High School.

Previously, I received a BS and MS in Computer Science from Stanford University. As an undergrad, I served on the residential student staff at Roble Hall, studied abroad in Oxford and Edinburgh, and worked with the Silicon Valley Urban Debate League to establish and run debate programs in low-income Peninsula high schools. It was also a time of deep confusion, exploration, soul-searching, and growth. I had the opportunity to speak about that experience.

I spent much of my master's focused on research. A stab at summarizing my absurdly broad research interests: through improvements in tools and algorithms, I use software to help non-practitioners leverage machine learning. I had the privilege of working with Alex Aiken, Vijay Pande, and Nick McKeown on projects under this umbrella.

Even before that, I graduated from Bellarmine College Preparatory in San Jose (Go Bells!). I doubt any high school students will come across this page, but if you do, I strongly encourage that you get involved in forensics, particularly policy debate. It has changed and shaped my life more than anything else I have done.
